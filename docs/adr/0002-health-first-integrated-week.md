# ADR-0002: Health-First Integrated Week

Date: 2026-06-08

## Status

Accepted for seed phase.

## Context

The user has two fixed weekly activities:

- Wednesday night 5K.
- Thursday hot yoga.

The user also clarified that the training goal is health, stability, mobility, and heart health. Muscle mass and peak strength are secondary.

## Decision

Use a weekly plan with:

- Two focused calisthenics sessions: Monday and Friday.
- One fixed 5K: Wednesday night.
- One fixed hot yoga session: Thursday.
- Two to three easy cardio or walking sessions to reach the weekly heart-health target.
- Daily mobility or movement floor.

Calisthenics progression prioritizes movement quality, balance, range of motion, and control before reps, leverage, or load.

## Consequences

Positive:

- Keeps existing commitments central.
- Avoids stacking hard calisthenics on 5K night.
- Makes cardio volume easier to adjust without forcing more running.
- Aligns strength work with health and joint durability rather than hypertrophy.

Tradeoffs:

- Strength gains may be slower than a three-day strength plan.
- Running performance may improve slowly because speed work is intentionally deferred.
- Hot yoga is treated as a training stress but not automatically counted toward cardio totals.

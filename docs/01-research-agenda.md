# Research Agenda

## Seed Questions

1. What is the minimum effective weekly mix of aerobic and strengthening activity for general adult health?
2. How should a daily calisthenics habit be structured so that the routine is daily but hard strength work is not overdone?
3. Which no-equipment or low-equipment movements best cover squat, hinge, push, pull, trunk, and locomotion patterns?
4. What progression rule is simple enough to follow from logs?
5. What log fields predict whether the routine is sustainable?
6. What equipment would most improve the baseline with the least complexity?
7. What running volume best supports heart health when the fixed anchor is a Wednesday night 5K?
8. How should Thursday hot yoga be counted: cardio, mobility, recovery, or a separate heat-stress practice?

## Working Hypotheses

- A daily habit anchor improves adherence, but full-body resistance work should be harder on only 2 to 3 nonconsecutive days at first.
- The seed routine should bias toward clean form, joint control, balance, mobility, repeatability, and leaving reps in reserve.
- The biggest early equipment gap is a safe pulling option: resistance band, suspension trainer, rings, or pull-up bar.
- The fixed Wednesday 5K plus one additional easy aerobic session should be enough running to start; remaining cardio can come from brisk walking, biking, or other low-impact work.
- Hot yoga supports mobility, balance, stress management, and heat tolerance practice, but it should not be used as the only cardio source unless the log shows sustained moderate or vigorous intensity.
- The routine should not change until at least 14 days of logs show whether it is too easy, too hard, or poorly balanced.

## Evidence Tasks

- Compare beginner bodyweight programs from credible organizations.
- Research pulling options for home calisthenics.
- Research warm-up, mobility, and cool-down conventions for bodyweight training.
- Research simple progression models: reps, rounds, tempo, range of motion, leverage, and rest.
- Research minimum dose versus optimal dose for strength, mobility, and cardio.
- Research heart-health running dose, including where current evidence becomes uncertain.
- Research hot yoga safety, hydration, and how to sequence it after the Wednesday 5K.

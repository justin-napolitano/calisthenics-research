# Baseline Routine

Date seeded: 2026-06-08

This is the first integrated routine, not the final program. Its job is to create a daily practice that can be logged, reviewed, and improved.

Primary goals:

- Heart health.
- Stability.
- Mobility.
- Durable joints and tissues.
- Whole-body strength maintenance.

Secondary goals:

- Muscle mass.
- Peak strength.
- Advanced calisthenics skills.

## Weekly Structure

Fixed anchors:

- Wednesday night: dedicated 5K.
- Thursday: hot yoga.

Weekly structure:

| Day | Primary Work | Secondary Work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | Calisthenics A | Easy walk if desired |
| Tuesday | Easy aerobic work | Short mobility |
| Wednesday | Night 5K | Warm-up and cooldown only |
| Thursday | Hot yoga | Hydration and recovery check |
| Friday | Calisthenics B | Easy walk if desired |
| Saturday | Easy aerobic work | Short mobility |
| Sunday | Recovery walk and mobility | Optional balance practice |

The daily habit is allowed every day. Hard calisthenics and hard running are not.

## Daily Minimum

Do this even on easy days:

1. Warm up for 3 to 5 minutes.
2. Do the morning floor or the day's primary work.
3. Log the session.

## Morning Floor

Cap: 15 minutes.

Do this in the morning most days. It should feel like preparation, not training fatigue.

1. Spine wake-up: cat-cow, 2 minutes.
2. Down-dog plank push-up flow, 4 minutes.
3. Bodyweight squat pattern, 3 minutes.
4. Front plank or bird-dog/side-plank core block, 4 minutes.
5. Standing Calf Raises - Wall, 2 minutes.

The detailed version, including ACE exercise reference URLs, lives in [Morning Floor V0.2](protocols/morning-floor-v0.2.md).

Safety rule:

If known osteoporosis, osteopenia, fracture history, unexplained bone pain, or high fall risk is present, keep the protocol conservative and get clinician guidance before adding harder loading.

## Warm-Up

Move at low intensity and build gradually:

- 6 to 8 cat-cow reps.
- 2 slow rounds of downward-facing dog to front plank.
- 6 to 10 bodyweight squats.
- 10 Standing Calf Raises - Wall reps.

## Targeted Modules

These are optional 6 to 10 minute blocks, not extra full workouts. Add them only when the personal A/B sessions, Wednesday 5K, and Thursday hot yoga are recovering cleanly.

### Core Stability And Moving Plank

Use 1 to 2 times per week.

- Hip Rotations (Push-up Position): 2 x 5-8 each side.
- CKC Parascapular Exercises: 2 x 2-4 holds of 5-10 seconds each side.
- Modified side plank: 2 x 20-30 seconds each side.

### Back And Posture

Use 1 to 2 times per week.

- Downward-Facing Dog: 2 x 30-45 seconds.
- Bird-dog: 2 x 8 each side.

### Legs, Bone, And Balance

Use 1 time per week if running recovery is clean. Avoid the day before the Wednesday 5K.

- Forward lunge: 2 x 6-10 each side.
- Standing Calf Raises - Wall: 3 x 15-20.

## Easy Cardio And Recovery Days

Pick one:

- Easy run, walk-jog, bike, or brisk walk.
- A 10 to 30 minute walk.
- One easy round of the ACE-linked morning floor.
- One easy round of Calisthenics A or B at roughly half effort.

Keep easy days easy. They exist to build aerobic volume, preserve the daily routine, and support recovery.

## Effort Rule

End each set with clean form and at least 2 to 4 reps in reserve. Most calisthenics work should feel like 6 to 7 out of 10. Do not train to failure in the seed phase.

For the current personal tier, the volume cap is recovery-based:

- Keep A2's 50 push-ups and 50 squats only if Wednesday running and Thursday hot yoga stay normal.
- Reduce the next strength session by 25 to 40 percent if soreness changes running, yoga, sleep, or normal movement.
- Do not add targeted modules during a week when the full-body sessions already feel like enough.

## Health-First Progression Bias

Progress movement quality before load:

1. Smoother reps.
2. Better range of motion.
3. Better balance and control.
4. More consistent weekly completion.
5. More reps or harder variations.

Do not add harder variations if they reduce mobility, balance, or recovery.

## Stop Signals

Stop the movement or session if there is sharp pain, chest pain, dizziness, faintness, unusual shortness of breath, numbness, or a joint sensation that feels unstable.

## First Review Date

Review the baseline after 14 logged days, or earlier if soreness, pain, or fatigue is trending worse.

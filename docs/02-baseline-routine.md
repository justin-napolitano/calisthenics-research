# Baseline Routine

Date seeded: 2026-06-08

This is the first routine, not the final program. Its job is to create a daily practice that can be logged, reviewed, and improved.

## Weekly Structure

Hard strength days:

- Monday
- Wednesday
- Friday

Easy baseline days:

- Tuesday
- Thursday
- Saturday
- Sunday

The daily habit is allowed every day. The hard full-body circuit is not.

## Daily Minimum

Do this even on easy days:

1. Warm up for 3 to 5 minutes.
2. Do either one easy circuit or a mobility/recovery block.
3. Log the session.

## Warm-Up

Move at low intensity and build gradually:

- 60 seconds marching, brisk walking, or step-ups.
- 10 arm circles forward and backward.
- 10 hip hinges.
- 10 bodyweight good-mornings or slow squats.
- 10 scapular wall slides or shoulder blade squeezes.

## Hard-Day Circuit

Do 2 rounds in week 1. Use 3 rounds only if the first two rounds feel easy, clean, and recoverable.

Rest 45 to 90 seconds between movements as needed.

| Pattern | Movement | Starting Dose | Easier | Harder |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Squat | Bodyweight squat | 8-12 reps | Chair squat | Slow tempo squat |
| Push | Incline push-up | 6-12 reps | Wall push-up | Floor push-up |
| Hinge | Glute bridge | 10-15 reps | Short range bridge | Single-leg bridge |
| Single leg | Reverse lunge | 6-10 each side | Split squat to short depth | Deeper reverse lunge |
| Pull/posterior | Band row, table row, or prone W raise | 8-12 reps | Prone W raise | Safer loaded row option |
| Trunk | Forearm plank | 20-45 seconds | Elevated plank | Longer plank |
| Lateral trunk | Side plank | 10-30 seconds each side | Knees bent | Full side plank |
| Lower leg | Calf raise | 10-20 reps | Hands supported | Single-leg calf raise |

## Easy Baseline Day

Pick one:

- One easy round of the circuit at roughly half effort.
- A 10 to 30 minute walk.
- A 10 minute mobility block: hips, ankles, thoracic spine, shoulders.

Keep easy days easy. They exist to preserve the daily routine and support recovery.

## Effort Rule

End each set with clean form and at least 2 to 4 reps in reserve. Do not train to failure in the seed phase.

## Stop Signals

Stop the movement or session if there is sharp pain, chest pain, dizziness, faintness, unusual shortness of breath, numbness, or a joint sensation that feels unstable.

## First Review Date

Review the baseline after 14 logged days, or earlier if soreness, pain, or fatigue is trending worse.

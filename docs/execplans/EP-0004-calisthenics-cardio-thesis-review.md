# EP-0004: Calisthenics And Cardio Thesis Review

Status: active
Date: 2026-06-08

## Goal

Ground the user's health-first cardio and calisthenics plan before returning to the self-defense and equilibrium framework.

## Core Questions

1. What does "optimal" mean for heart-health cardio when the user has a fixed Wednesday night 5K?
2. What is the best starting dose for calisthenics when health, stability, and mobility outrank mass and max strength?
3. How should the plan balance cardio, calisthenics, hot yoga, and recovery?
4. What should be tested in logs before increasing volume or intensity?

- [Cardio thesis](../research/cardio/2026-06-08-cardio-thesis.md)
- [Calisthenics thesis](../research/calisthenics/2026-06-08-calisthenics-thesis.md)

## Source Domains

- Public health physical activity guidelines.
- Heart-health and mortality dose-response evidence.
- Running-specific dose-response evidence.
- Resistance training guidelines.
- Balance, mobility, flexibility, and neuromotor training guidance.
- Recovery and injury-risk literature.

## Deliverables

- Cardio thesis.
- Calisthenics thesis.
- Source notes for each.
- Critical review of the first 4-week test.
- Decision note for the next routine version.

## Acceptance Criteria

- "Optimal" is defined as benefit-to-burden, not maximal volume.
- The Wednesday 5K and Thursday hot yoga are built into the plan.
- Every numeric target is labeled as guideline-backed, evidence-informed, or experimental.
- Progression rules depend on logs, not ambition.
- Self-defense research remains deferred until this health baseline is grounded.

## Non-Goals

- Publishing the Astro site.
- Designing the database.
- Building self-defense technique cards.
- Claiming a universal optimal dose for everyone.

# EP-0003: Equilibrium Literature Review

Status: deferred pending EP-0004
Date: 2026-06-08

## Goal

Ground the movement thesis in literature before building the public site, database, or movement atom implementation.

This work is deferred until EP-0004 grounds the cardio and calisthenics baseline.

## Core Thesis To Test

Human conflict movement can be studied as control of equilibrium under pressure: preserve my own balance, posture, perception, and mobility while disrupting the other person's balance, posture, base, timing, perception, or recovery options enough to escape, put them on the ground, or immobilize them.

## Source Domains

1. Kinesiology and balance science.
2. Motor learning and ecological dynamics.
3. Judo biomechanics and kuzushi.
4. Aikido interaction, nonviolence, and redirection.
5. Grappling, boxing, Muay Thai, and pressure-tested combat sports.
6. Ethics, safety, de-escalation, and self-defense boundaries.

## Deliverables

- Source notes for each domain.
- Claim ledger entries for the revised thesis.
- Critical review of the strongest and weakest claims.
- Vocabulary glossary.
- A decision note about whether to proceed with site and database implementation.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Every top-level thesis claim is marked as supported, partially supported, assumption, or rejected.
- Aikido-inspired claims are separated from pressure-tested combat claims.
- Balance/equilibrium definitions cite balance-control literature.
- Judo kuzushi claims cite judo biomechanics literature.
- Training recommendations are deferred until safety and resistance boundaries are explicit.

## Non-Goals

- Building the Astro site.
- Designing the database schema.
- Publishing public self-defense advice.
- Creating technique cards.

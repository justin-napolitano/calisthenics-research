# First 14 Days Plan

Date: 2026-06-08

## Objective

Run the integrated seed baseline for 14 days without optimizing early. The output should be a useful body of logs, not a perfect routine.

## Schedule

| Day | Type | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | calisthenics | Calisthenics A, 2 rounds |
| Tuesday | cardio | 30-40 minutes easy walk, walk-jog, or bike |
| Wednesday | run | Dedicated night 5K |
| Thursday | hot_yoga | Hot yoga, hydration, and heat-response log |
| Friday | calisthenics | Calisthenics B, 2 rounds |
| Saturday | cardio | 30-45 minutes easy run, walk-jog, bike, or long walk |
| Sunday | recovery | 30-60 minute walk plus mobility |

Repeat the pattern for week 2.

## Success Criteria

- At least 12 of 14 days logged.
- Both Wednesday 5Ks completed or explicitly explained.
- Both Thursday hot yoga sessions logged.
- At least 3 of 4 calisthenics sessions completed.
- At least 150 moderate-equivalent cardio minutes in each week.
- No repeated sharp pain signal.
- Average calisthenics effort stays at or below 7 out of 10.
- Soreness does not interfere with normal movement for more than 48 hours.

## Change Rules During The Trial

Allowed:

- Regress a movement immediately if form breaks or pain appears.
- Skip or shorten calisthenics if recovery is clearly poor.
- Replace an unsafe pulling setup with prone W raises or band rows.
- Convert Saturday running to walking or biking if lower-leg soreness appears.
- Treat hot yoga as a training stress and reduce Friday volume if needed.

Not allowed:

- Add extra hard days.
- Chase max reps.
- Add speed work.
- Add advanced skills.
- Rewrite the program because one session felt easy.

## Day 14 Review

Answer these before changing the routine:

- What was the adherence rate?
- How many moderate-equivalent cardio minutes were logged each week?
- Did Wednesday 5K feel sustainable or race-like?
- Did Thursday hot yoga compromise Friday recovery?
- Which movement limited the workout most?
- Which movement felt undertrained?
- Did any pain signal repeat?
- Did easy days stay easy?
- Should the next change be more cardio minutes, better pulling equipment, a mobility change, or a small calisthenics progression?

# First 14 Days Plan

Date: 2026-06-08

## Objective

Run the seed baseline for 14 days without optimizing early. The output should be a useful body of logs, not a perfect routine.

## Schedule

| Day | Type | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | hard | 2 rounds of the hard-day circuit |
| Tuesday | easy | walk, mobility, or one easy circuit |
| Wednesday | hard | 2 rounds of the hard-day circuit |
| Thursday | easy | walk, mobility, or one easy circuit |
| Friday | hard | 2 rounds of the hard-day circuit |
| Saturday | easy | walk, mobility, or one easy circuit |
| Sunday | easy | walk, mobility, or one easy circuit |

Repeat the pattern for week 2.

## Success Criteria

- At least 12 of 14 days logged.
- At least 5 of 6 hard days completed.
- No repeated sharp pain signal.
- Average hard-day effort stays at or below 7 out of 10.
- Soreness does not interfere with normal movement for more than 48 hours.

## Change Rules During The Trial

Allowed:

- Regress a movement immediately if form breaks or pain appears.
- Skip a hard day if recovery is clearly poor.
- Replace an unsafe pulling setup with prone W raises or band rows.

Not allowed:

- Add extra hard days.
- Chase max reps.
- Add advanced skills.
- Rewrite the program because one session felt easy.

## Day 14 Review

Answer these before changing the routine:

- What was the adherence rate?
- Which movement limited the workout most?
- Which movement felt undertrained?
- Did any pain signal repeat?
- Did easy days stay easy?
- Should the next change be more reps, one extra round, a harder variation, or better pulling equipment?

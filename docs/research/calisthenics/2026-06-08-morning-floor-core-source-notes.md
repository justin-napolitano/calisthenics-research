# Source Notes: Morning Floor And Core

Date: 2026-06-08

## Search Goal

Build a 15-minute morning floor that includes cat-cow, a down-dog/plank/push-up flow, squat work, and core strength while staying compatible with the health-first baseline.

## Sources

### Mayo Clinic: Core exercises

Source: https://www.mayoclinic.org/healthy-lifestyle/fitness/in-depth/core-strength/art-20546851

Mayo Clinic presents core exercises such as bridge and plank-style movements and emphasizes breathing during core exercises. It also advises clinician guidance for people with back problems, osteoporosis, or other health concerns.

Use in thesis:

- Include plank/bridge-style core work.
- Do not hold breath during bracing work.
- Keep the safety boundary around back issues and osteoporosis.

### Mayo Clinic: Balanced fitness routine

Source: https://sportsmedicine.mayoclinic.org/news/mayo-clinic-q-and-a-5-elements-of-a-balanced-fitness-routine/

Mayo describes a balanced fitness routine as including aerobic fitness, strength training, core exercises, balance training, and flexibility/stretching.

Use in thesis:

- The morning floor can combine core, balance, mobility, and light strength.
- The morning floor should not replace focused strength or aerobic work.

### Harvard Health: Three moves for better spine health

Source: https://www.health.harvard.edu/healthy-aging-and-longevity/three-moves-for-better-spine-health

Harvard Health discusses multiple core/spine stability exercises, including bird-dog.

Use in thesis:

- Add lateral core and bird-dog work, not only front planks.
- Treat core strength as spine control and endurance, not just abdominal fatigue.

### Core Stability Training for Injury Prevention

Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3806175/

This review discusses core stability, endurance, and stabilization exercises.

Use in thesis:

- Track core improvement through control, endurance, and symmetry.
- Include anti-extension, lateral stability, posterior chain, and quadruped control.

## Provisional Interpretation

The morning floor should become a repeatable sequence:

1. Spine preparation: cat-cow.
2. Yoga/plank flow: down dog to plank to push-up variation.
3. Squat pattern: bodyweight squat or squat hold.
4. Core strength: rotate front plank, lateral stability, and bird-dog work.
5. Standing Calf Raises - Wall finish.

Core improvement should be measured with submaximal quality benchmarks, not daily max holds.

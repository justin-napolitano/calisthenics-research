# Source Notes: Bone Health

Date: 2026-06-08

## Search Goal

Answer whether running and cardio are enough to address age-related bone loss, and update the daily floor and calisthenics thesis accordingly.

## Sources

### NIAMS: Exercise For Your Bone Health

Source: https://www.niams.nih.gov/health-topics/exercise-your-bone-health

NIAMS says exercise can help prevent bone loss in adults, improve balance and coordination, and help prevent falls and fractures. It identifies a combination of weight-bearing exercise, resistance training, and balance training as best for building and maintaining healthy bones.

Use in thesis:

- Running and brisk walking count as weight-bearing exercise.
- Resistance training and balance work are still required; cardio alone is not the bone-health plan.
- If low bone density, osteoporosis, or physical limitations exist, exercise selection should be clinician-guided.

### Bone Health And Osteoporosis Foundation: Weight-Bearing And Muscle-Strengthening

Source: https://www.bonehealthandosteoporosis.org/preventing-fractures/exercise-to-stay-healthy/weight-bearing/

BHOF distinguishes weight-bearing and muscle-strengthening exercises as important for bone density. It lists jogging/running, fast walking, stair climbing, and resistance work as examples.

Use in thesis:

- Supports keeping running/walking but pairing it with strengthening.
- Supports bodyweight and resistance-band work as part of the bone-health stack.

### International Osteoporosis Foundation: Exercise For Individuals With Osteoporosis

Source: https://www.osteoporosis.foundation/health-professionals/prevention/exercise/exercise-individuals-with-osteoporosis

IOF describes progressive resistance training, aerobic physical activity, and balance/multicomponent programs as relevant for people with osteoporosis, while emphasizing individualization and health-professional involvement.

Use in thesis:

- Supports progressive resistance and balance as bone/fall-risk priorities.
- Reinforces safety boundary for anyone with known osteoporosis or fracture history.

### Mayo Clinic Press: Exercising When You Have Osteoporosis

Source: https://mcpress.mayoclinic.org/healthy-aging/exercising-when-you-have-osteoporosis/

Mayo Clinic Press identifies weight-bearing exercise, resistance exercise, and back-strengthening extension exercise as common recommendations for osteoporosis, and notes that resistance training should be appropriate to the person's degree of bone loss.

Use in thesis:

- Supports posture/back-extension work as a morning-floor candidate.
- Supports avoiding generic high-impact advice if bone loss is already present.

### MedlinePlus: Exercise, Lifestyle, And Your Bones

Source: https://medlineplus.gov/ency/patientinstructions/000492.htm

MedlinePlus recommends weight-bearing exercises 3 or more days per week for a total of more than 90 minutes per week to protect bones, and includes careful weight training as a bone-supporting activity.

Use in thesis:

- Gives a concrete target for weight-bearing exposure.
- Supports tracking weight-bearing minutes separately from total cardio minutes.

## Provisional Interpretation

Running and brisk walking are useful for bone because they are weight-bearing. But they are not enough by themselves for a complete bone-health plan. The plan should include:

- weight-bearing aerobic activity
- progressive resistance training
- posture/back-extension work
- balance training
- safe progression based on bone status and symptoms

Cycling and swimming can support heart health but should not be counted as primary bone-loading work.

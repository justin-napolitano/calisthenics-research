# Source Notes: Calisthenics Thesis

Date: 2026-06-08

## Search Goal

Ground a health-first calisthenics thesis where stability, mobility, and function outrank mass and max strength.

## Sources

### HHS Physical Activity Guidelines

Source: https://health.gov/paguidelines/second-edition/pdf/Physical_Activity_Guidelines_2nd_edition.pdf

HHS recommends muscle-strengthening activities of moderate or greater intensity involving all major muscle groups on 2 or more days per week.

Use in thesis:

- Establishes the minimum weekly strengthening floor.
- Supports full-body pattern coverage.

### ACSM 2026 Resistance Training Guidance

Source: https://acsm.org/resistance-training-guidelines-update-2026/

ACSM's 2026 update emphasizes training all major muscle groups at least twice per week and says bodyweight, elastic bands, and home-based routines can yield meaningful benefits.

Use in thesis:

- Supports bodyweight training as a legitimate tool.
- Supports a simple plan over a complex "perfect" plan.

### ACSM 2026 Infographic

Source: https://www.acsm.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Resistance-Training-Position-Stand-infographic.pdf

The ACSM infographic summarizes the 2026 position stand and says to train all major muscle groups at least 2 days per week and build gradually over time.

Use in thesis:

- Supports the two-session starting structure.
- Supports gradual progression.

### WHO Physical Activity Guidelines

Source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/n/who336656/ch4/

WHO guidelines include muscle-strengthening recommendations and, for older adults, multicomponent physical activity emphasizing functional balance and strength training.

Use in thesis:

- Supports integrating balance and functional strength as health-relevant capacities.
- Exact balance dose for younger adults still needs better sourcing.

### CDC Older Adult Multicomponent Guidance

Source: https://www.cdc.gov/physical-activity-basics/adding-older-adults/what-counts.html

CDC explains multicomponent physical activity as combining aerobic, muscle-strengthening, and balance activities, with examples such as yoga and tai chi.

Use in thesis:

- Supports treating balance and mobility as first-class physical capacities.
- This source is older-adult-specific, so use carefully for general adult framing.

## Provisional Interpretation

For this repo, the first calisthenics thesis should test 2 focused full-body sessions per week plus short daily mobility and balance exposure. That dose is guideline-compatible, easy to sustain around cardio and hot yoga, and can be progressed only if logs show clean recovery.

# Source Notes: Cardio Thesis

Date: 2026-06-08

## Search Goal

Ground the cardio thesis around "optimal for heart health" without pretending there is one exact universal dose.

## Sources

### HHS Physical Activity Guidelines

Source: https://health.gov/paguidelines/second-edition/pdf/Physical_Activity_Guidelines_2nd_edition.pdf

The U.S. guideline range for adults is 150 to 300 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity, 75 to 150 minutes per week of vigorous aerobic activity, or an equivalent combination. Adults should also perform muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle groups on 2 or more days per week.

Use in thesis:

- Establishes the guideline floor and upper guideline range.
- Supports mixed moderate and vigorous activity.

### American Heart Association Recommendations

Source: https://www.heart.org/en/healthy-living/exercise-and-physical-activity/fitness-basics/aha-recs-for-physical-activity-in-adults

AHA uses the same adult aerobic and strength targets and notes additional benefit at 300 minutes per week.

Use in thesis:

- Supports a heart-health framing.
- Supports building toward, but not necessarily starting at, 300 minutes.

### WHO Physical Activity Guidelines

Source: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240014886

WHO recommendations align with 150 to 300 moderate or 75 to 150 vigorous minutes per week for adults and emphasize limiting sedentary behavior.

Use in thesis:

- Cross-checks that U.S. recommendations are not idiosyncratic.
- Supports treating total weekly activity as a health target.

### Lee et al., 2022, Circulation

Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9378548/

This prospective cohort study reports additional mortality benefit among participants doing 2 to 4 times the minimum recommended dose of moderate or vigorous leisure-time physical activity, compared with those merely meeting the guidelines.

Use in thesis:

- Supports testing above-minimum volume if recovery and adherence are good.
- Does not prove that everyone should immediately target high volume.

### Arem et al., 2015, JAMA Internal Medicine

Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4451435/

This pooled analysis found mortality benefit below, at, and above the minimum physical activity recommendation, with larger benefit around 2 to 3 times the minimum and a threshold around 3 to 5 times the minimum.

Use in thesis:

- Supports dose-response thinking.
- Reinforces that "optimal" likely means a range with diminishing returns, not a single number.

## Provisional Interpretation

For this repo, the best initial target is 180 to 240 moderate-equivalent minutes per week, because it exceeds the minimum while remaining more realistic than jumping directly to 300 minutes. If 4 weeks of logs show clean recovery and high adherence, the next test can move toward 240 to 300 minutes.

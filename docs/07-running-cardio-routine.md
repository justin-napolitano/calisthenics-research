# Running And Cardio Routine

Date seeded: 2026-06-08

## Goal

Build a cardio routine that is optimal enough for heart health and easy enough to keep. The plan starts from two fixed anchors:

- Wednesday night 5K.
- Thursday hot yoga.

## Weekly Cardio Target

Working target:

- 150 to 300 moderate-equivalent minutes per week.
- Or 75 to 150 vigorous minutes per week.
- Or a combination where 1 vigorous minute counts roughly like 2 moderate minutes.

Practical seed target:

- 180 to 240 moderate-equivalent minutes per week.
- 1 fixed 5K run.
- 1 additional easy aerobic session.
- 2 to 3 walks or low-impact cardio sessions.

This is a health target, not a race-training target.

## Weekly Plan

| Day | Cardio Role | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | Optional easy movement | 10-30 minute walk |
| Tuesday | Easy aerobic base | 30-40 minute brisk walk, walk-jog, or bike |
| Wednesday | Fixed 5K | 5K at sustainable effort |
| Thursday | Hot yoga | Count separately unless sustained cardio intensity is clear |
| Friday | Optional easy movement | 10-30 minute walk |
| Saturday | Easy aerobic base | 30-45 minute easy run, walk-jog, bike, or long walk |
| Sunday | Recovery aerobic | 30-60 minute walk |

## Running Dose

Start with:

- Wednesday 5K.
- Saturday easy run or walk-jog for 30 to 45 minutes.

If Saturday running creates soreness, joint pain, or dread, make it a walk-jog or brisk walk. The heart-health goal does not require every cardio minute to be running.

Optional later progression:

- Add a short 20 to 30 minute easy run on Tuesday only after 4 weeks of clean logs.
- Keep the Wednesday 5K as the only hard run unless a later goal requires speed work.

## Intensity Rules

Easy aerobic work:

- You can talk in full sentences.
- Effort is 4 to 6 out of 10.
- Finish feeling like you could continue.

5K night:

- Sustainable effort beats racing every week.
- If Wednesday becomes a hard run, keep Tuesday and Saturday easy.
- Warm up before and cool down after.

Hot yoga:

- Treat as mobility, balance, stress-management, and heat exposure.
- Count it as cardio only if the log shows sustained moderate or vigorous breathing/heart-rate intensity.
- Hydrate before, during, and after.
- Stop if dizzy, lightheaded, nauseated, unusually weak, or overheated.

## Progression Rules

- Increase only one cardio variable at a time: frequency, duration, or intensity.
- Add 5 to 10 minutes to one easy session only after a clean week.
- Do not add speed work during the first 14 days.
- If lower-leg pain, joint pain, or unusual fatigue appears, reduce running first and keep walking.

## Review Metrics

Track weekly:

- Total moderate-equivalent cardio minutes.
- Running minutes.
- Walking or low-impact cardio minutes.
- Wednesday 5K effort.
- Saturday cardio type.
- Hot yoga recovery response.
- Any foot, ankle, knee, hip, back, or heat-stress signal.

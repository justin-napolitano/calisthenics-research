# Intent

## Purpose

Create a durable personal workspace for movement practice, exercise research, daily logs, routine planning, and a future structured database once the vocabulary is grounded.

## Immediate Goal

Seed a health-first weekly baseline around calisthenics, running, cardio, hot yoga, and later martial arts practice. The first version should require little or no equipment, train the major movement patterns, support heart health and mobility, and be simple enough to log consistently.

The larger goal is to remove mystical or tradition-only explanations from martial and health practice by reducing skills to observable movement problems grounded in physics, physiology, constraints, and evidence.

## Scope

In scope:

- Daily movement logs.
- Research notes about bodyweight training, running, recovery, mobility, hot yoga, grappling, boxing, Muay Thai, and progression.
- Baseline routines and changes over time.
- Personal observations about adherence, soreness, effort, and exercise response.
- Structured claim and source records.
- Structured movement, skill, constraint, and transfer records after the research vocabulary stabilizes.
- Public Astro site content derived from Markdown and structured records.

Out of scope for now:

- Nutrition planning.
- Medical diagnosis or injury rehabilitation.
- Wearable integrations.
- Automated dashboards.
- Advanced skills such as handstands, muscle-ups, levers, or planches.

Deferred, not rejected:

- Database-backed movement graph.
- Skill decomposition tooling.
- Video annotation.
- Wearable data ingestion.
- Public claim and evidence browser.

## Safety Boundary

The repo can help organize evidence and logs. It cannot decide whether a symptom is safe. Pain, dizziness, chest symptoms, faintness, or injury-specific questions should be treated as stop signals or clinician questions.

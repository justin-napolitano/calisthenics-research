# Morning Floor V0.2

Date: 2026-06-08

Status: active test

## Goal

Create a repeatable morning practice that improves mobility, balance, posture, core strength, and low-dose bone-loading without creating training fatigue.

Cap: 15 minutes.

## Protocol

### 1. Spine Wake-Up: 2 minutes

- Cat-cow: 6 to 8 slow reps. Use the ACE reference for setup and spinal sequencing.

Intent:

- Move the spine gently.
- Start breathing.
- Notice stiffness without forcing range.

### 2. Down-Dog Plank Push-Up Flow: 4 minutes

Repeat 3 to 5 slow rounds:

1. Downward-facing dog, 2 breaths.
2. High plank or front plank, 1 slow breath.
3. Push-up variation, 1 to 5 clean reps.
4. Return to down dog.

Push-up options:

- Bent-knee push-up.
- Push-up.

Intent:

- Train shoulders, trunk, pushing strength, and hamstring/calf length.
- Keep ribs and pelvis controlled in plank.
- Stop the push-up set before form breaks.

### 3. Squat Pattern: 3 minutes

Choose one:

- Bodyweight squat: 6 to 10 clean reps.

Intent:

- Keep hips, ankles, and knees working daily.
- Add gentle weight-bearing stimulus.
- Track morning mobility.

### 4. Core Block: 4 minutes

Alternate A and B by day.

Core A:

- Front plank: 15 to 45 seconds.

Core B:

- Bird-dog: 4 to 8 each side.
- Modified side plank: 10 to 30 seconds each side.

Intent:

- Build core strength as control.
- Train anti-extension, anti-rotation, lateral trunk, and posterior-chain coordination.
- Breathe through the work.

### 5. Bone-Loading And Mobility Finish: 2 minutes

Choose one bone-loading option:

- Standing Calf Raises - Wall: 10 to 20 reps.

## Progression Rules

Do not progress everything at once.

Progress order:

1. Complete 5 mornings in a week.
2. Improve smoothness and breathing.
3. Add reps inside the range.
4. Add time inside the range.
5. Use a harder variation.

## Core Strength Benchmarks

Test weekly, not daily:

- Front plank: 45 seconds with quiet breathing and no low-back sag.
- Modified side plank: 30 seconds per side with steady hips.
- Bird-dog: 8 controlled reps per side without trunk rotation.

Passing a benchmark means it looked clean and felt controlled. It does not require maximal effort.

## Stop Or Regress

Regress if:

- low-back pain appears
- wrist or shoulder pain appears
- plank form collapses
- breath holding becomes necessary
- morning floor starts affecting the day's main training

title: Morning Floor
description: Research project for the daily 10-15 minute baseline routine.
---

## Thesis

A short morning practice can make mobility, push/squat exposure, core strength, posture, balance, and bone-loading consistent without adding training fatigue.

## Current Hypothesis

A 10-15 minute morning floor built from cat-cow, down-dog/plank/push-up flow, squat patterning, alternating core blocks, and calf raises is enough to support the baseline if it stays easy and repeatable.

## Metrics

- Completion days.
- Duration.
- Push-up variation.
- Squat comfort.
- Core block A/B.
- Posture/back response.
- Joint response.

## Falsifiers

- It feels like another workout.
- Joint pain appears.
- Duration makes it hard to do daily.
- Logs show no useful signal.

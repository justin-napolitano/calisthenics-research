## Current Understanding

The calisthenics baseline should cover push, squat, single-leg control, trunk control, posture/back work, balance, and low-dose bone-loading. Pulling and external load are limited without equipment, so pull-up equipment and kettlebells belong in a separate equipment layer.

## Current Exercise Rule

Active exercises should have direct ACE exercise-library references unless they are explicitly marked as a research candidate.

## Open Questions

- Which intermediate A/B movements become mainstays after four weeks?
- Does the equipment layer improve back/posture and loading without harming running recovery?
- Does one weekly supine block improve core and posterior-chain work without reducing adherence?
